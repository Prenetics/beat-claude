## The Challenge

You are the newly hired Creative Strategist at IM8 Health. Your first assignment: audit everything IM8 is putting into the world creatively, identify what's working and what's not, build a 30-day creative sprint plan, and show us how AI supercharges your strategy process.

**Everything you need is publicly available.** IM8's ad library, social content, website, and competitors are all fair game.

## Part 1: Creative Audit (35%)

### What to do:

Conduct a comprehensive creative audit of IM8's current advertising and organic content. Your audit should be structured across three layers:

### Layer 1: Paid Creative (Meta Ad Library + TikTok)

- **Ad Inventory:** Go to Meta Ad Library and search for IM8 Health. Document every active ad — format (static, video, carousel), messaging angle, CTA, and landing page destination.
- **Creative Themes:** Categorize the ads by messaging angle (e.g., ingredient story, founder/celebrity, social proof, lifestyle, educational, promotional). What's the current creative mix?
- **Hook Analysis:** For video ads, break down the first 3 seconds. What hooks are being used? Rate their likely effectiveness.
- **Landing Page Alignment:** Do the ads connect logically to the landing pages they link to? Is there message match?
- **Volume & Freshness:** How many active creatives are running? How often do new ads appear? Is IM8 testing enough creative volume?

### Layer 2: Organic Content (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube)

- **Content Mix:** Categorize organic content by format and theme. What's the posting cadence?
- **Performance Signals:** Using publicly visible engagement data, identify top and bottom performers. What patterns emerge?
- **Brand Consistency:** Is there a coherent brand voice and visual identity across platforms? Or does it feel fragmented?
- **Community & Comments:** What are people saying in the comments? What questions come up? What objections appear?

### Layer 3: Competitive Landscape

- **Competitor Creative Audit:** Pull ads from Meta Ad Library for 3–4 competitors (AG1, Ritual, Momentous, LMNT, Huel — pick the most relevant). Compare:
  - Creative volume and freshness
  - Messaging angles and hooks
  - Production quality and editing style
  - Use of founders, ambassadors, UGC, and social proof
- **IM8's Competitive Position:** Where does IM8 have a creative advantage? Where is it falling behind? What are competitors doing that IM8 should steal (or deliberately avoid)?

### Gap Analysis:

Synthesize your audit into a clear gap analysis. Identify the **top 5–7 creative opportunities** for IM8, ranked by potential impact. For each opportunity, explain why it matters and what you'd do about it.

### What we want to see:

- Specific ad references (screenshots, links, descriptions) — not vague generalizations
- Data-informed observations using publicly visible metrics
- A strategic eye that connects creative choices to business outcomes
- Genuine competitive intelligence, not surface-level comparisons

## Part 2: 30-Day Creative Sprint Plan (35%)

### What to do:

Design a 30-day creative sprint plan for IM8 Health. This plan should operationalize the insights from your audit into a concrete, executable creative strategy.

### Your plan must include:

#### Strategic Foundation

- **Core Messaging Pillars:** Define 3–4 messaging pillars that all creative should ladder up to (e.g., "92 nutrients, zero compromise" / "Backed by science, built by Beckham" / etc.)
- **Audience Segments:** Identify 2–3 target audience segments and the messaging angles most likely to resonate with each
- **Funnel Mapping:** Map your creative concepts to funnel stages — what creative for awareness? Consideration? Conversion? Retention?

#### The Sprint Calendar

- **Week-by-Week Plan:** Break the 30 days into weekly themes or focuses
- **Ad Concepts (minimum 10):** For each concept, provide:
  - Concept name and format (static, video, carousel, UGC, etc.)
  - Messaging angle and hook (the first 3 seconds or headline)
  - Target audience and funnel stage
  - Platform (Meta, TikTok, YouTube, organic, or multi-platform)
  - Brief creative direction (what's on screen, copy, CTA)
  - Why this will work (strategic rationale — what insight or trend does it tap into?)
- **Testing Framework:** How would you structure creative testing?
  - What variables are you testing (hooks, messaging, format, audience)?
  - How many variants per concept?
  - What are your success metrics and kill criteria?
  - How do you iterate on winners?

#### Creative Formats to Include (aim for variety):

- At least 2 video ad concepts (with hook + structure)
- At least 2 static/carousel ad concepts (with headline + visual direction)
- At least 1 UGC-style concept (with creator brief outline)
- At least 1 trend-native or culturally relevant concept
- At least 1 ambassador/celebrity creative concept (Beckham, Sabalenka, or Bearman)

#### Production & Workflow

- How would you brief this work to editors and designers?
- What's the production timeline for each content type?
- How do you maintain brand consistency across high volume?

### Consider these IM8-specific angles:

- David Beckham is a co-founder, not just an endorser — how does this change the creative strategy compared to a typical celebrity deal?
- IM8 has a 12-week RCT and NSF Certification — these are rare in the supplement industry. How do you make science sexy in performance creative?
- Subscription model means the creative strategy must consider both acquisition AND retention
- Premium positioning in a market full of discount-driven competitors — how do you communicate value without discounting?
- Aryna Sabalenka (WTA #1) and Oliver Bearman (F1 driver) are live ambassadors — what creative opportunities do their careers and fandoms unlock?

## Part 3: AI Integration (20%)

### What to do:

Demonstrate how AI is embedded in your creative strategy workflow. This is not theoretical — show us your actual process.

### We want to see:

- **Trend Research & Cultural Mining:** How do you use AI to surface trending formats, sounds, hooks, or cultural moments relevant to IM8?
- **Ad Concept Generation:** Show your actual prompts for brainstorming ad concepts. Include the AI output and explain how you filter, refine, and select from it.
- **Competitor Analysis at Scale:** Can AI help you monitor competitor ad libraries, track creative volume, or identify messaging patterns faster?
- **Copy & Hook Writing:** Show your prompts for generating ad copy, hooks, and CTAs. Include variants and explain your selection criteria.
- **Creative Brief Generation:** How do you use AI to draft briefs for editors, designers, and UGC creators?
- **Performance Pattern Recognition:** How would you use AI to analyze ad performance data and extract creative insights (e.g., "video ads with science claims in the hook outperform lifestyle hooks by 40%")?
- **Prompt Library:** Share 3–5 of your best prompts for creative strategy tasks, with context on when and how you use each one.

### Important:

A Creative Strategist at IM8 will use AI every single day. We're not asking if you know what AI is — we're asking if it's already part of how you think, work, and create. Show us prompts. Show us outputs. Show us how you go from AI-assisted brainstorm to polished creative brief. If AI isn't integrated into your workflow yet, start now — this challenge is a good reason to.
