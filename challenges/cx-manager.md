## Part 1: Team Structure & Performance (40%)

### Scenario

You've just been hired as CX Manager at IM8 Health. You're inheriting a team of **6 agents** spread across HK, UK, and US time zones, plus **Siena AI** handling first-touch responses. The team is functional but inconsistent — no standardized KPIs, no weekly rhythm, and one agent who's been underperforming for two months.

### What We Need From You

**A. KPI Framework**

Design the KPI dashboard for this team. Present it as a table with:

| KPI | Target | Measurement Frequency | Who Owns It | Why This Matters for IM8 |
|-----|--------|-----------------------|-------------|--------------------------|

Include metrics for:
- Individual agent performance
- Siena AI effectiveness
- Team-level service delivery
- Customer satisfaction / quality
- Subscription-specific metrics (retention, churn-saves, reactivation)

Don't just list generic CX metrics. Tell us why each KPI matters specifically for a premium DTC subscription wellness brand.

**B. Weekly Operating Rhythm**

Design the weekly operating cadence for a global CX team. Be specific:
- What meetings happen, when, who attends, what's the agenda?
- What async check-ins or reports exist?
- How does a Monday in Hong Kong connect to a Friday in New York?
- How do you keep a distributed team aligned without drowning them in meetings?

**C. Managing Underperformance**

You have one agent who's been underperforming for two months. QA scores are low, response times are slow, and the team has noticed. You don't know the root cause yet.

Walk us through exactly how you handle this:
- Week 1: What do you do?
- Week 2-4: What does the performance plan look like?
- What does "success" look like at 30, 60, 90 days?
- At what point do you make the call to exit someone, and how?

We're not looking for HR-speak. We want to see leadership judgment.

## Part 2: Product Launch Preparation (30%)

### Scenario

IM8 is launching a **new flavor of Daily Ultimate Essentials** in 3 weeks. Marketing is going big — ambassador content from David Beckham, paid media push, TikTok Shop activation. You're expecting a significant volume spike across all channels.

### What We Need From You

You have 3 weeks to get your CX operation launch-ready. Walk us through your preparation plan, covering:

**Knowledge & Training**
- How do you ensure every agent (and Siena AI) can speak confidently about the new product?
- What training do you build? What format? How do you verify comprehension?
- How do you handle the 48 hours before launch when final details are still changing?

**Siena AI Updates**
- What needs to change in Siena's knowledge base, workflows, and response templates?
- How do you test AI responses before launch?
- What's your decision framework for what Siena handles vs. what goes to a human agent?

**Macros, Templates & Tools**
- What pre-built responses do you create?
- What internal tools or dashboards do you set up for launch week?

**Staffing & Scheduling**
- How do you adjust coverage for the volume spike?
- Do you bring in temp support? If so, how do you onboard them in 3 weeks?
- What does the launch-week schedule look like across time zones?

**Escalation Paths**
- What gets escalated, to whom, and how fast?
- What's the escalation path for product quality issues vs. shipping issues vs. billing issues?

**Early Warning Signals**
- What are you monitoring in the first 24-48 hours?
- What metrics tell you something is going wrong before customers start complaining?
- What's your "pull the emergency brake" threshold?

Be specific. Give us the actual plan, not a list of things you'd think about.

## Part 3: Crisis Response (20%)

### Scenario

It's launch day. In the first 2 hours:

- **Ticket volume spikes 4x** above your forecasted peak
- A **portal bug** is preventing 15% of new subscribers from completing checkout — they're flooding your channels
- **Siena AI is escalating everything** — its confidence threshold is too low for the new product queries, so it's routing 80% of conversations to human agents instead of the expected 40%
- Your agents are **overwhelmed** — queue times are climbing, CSAT is dropping in real-time, and one agent just messaged you saying they need to step away

**You have 30 minutes.** Walk us through exactly what you do.

Be precise:
- Minute 0-5: What's your first move?
- Minute 5-15: What are you triaging, delegating, and communicating?
- Minute 15-30: What's stabilized, what's still in motion, who's doing what?

Then:
- What do you communicate to leadership, and when?
- What do you tell your team?
- What do you tell customers who are stuck in the portal bug?
- What's your post-crisis review process?

**We're evaluating your instincts under pressure, not your ability to write a crisis playbook in hindsight.** Write this like you're living it.
